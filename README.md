# Retail_Sales_Intelligence

RETAIL SALES INTELLIGENCE

Interactive Excel Dashboard & Analytics

Description

This repository features an end-to-end business intelligence solution designed to analyze four years of United States domestic retail sales. By processing over 1,480 individual transaction records, this project transforms raw data into a fully interactive Microsoft Excel dashboard. The analysis exposes the hidden realities behind headline revenue figures—identifying true profitability drivers, pinpointing margin-draining discount thresholds, and evaluating supply chain compliance to empower data-driven commercial decisions.

Tech Stack

Business Intelligence & Visualization: Microsoft Excel (PivotTables, Slicers, Dynamic Charts, and Conditional Formatting for the interactive dashboard).

Data Extraction & Transformation: Excel Native Functions (TRIM, CLEAN, AVERAGEIFS, VLOOKUP) for resolving text inconsistencies, imputing missing values, and shaping the structured tables.

Calculations & Statistical Logic: Advanced Excel Formulas (CORREL, STDEV.S, COUNTIFS) for creating dynamic KPIs, Z-score anomaly detection, and correlation analysis.

Source Data: Extracted and processed entirely within a single workbook (Sale combined (Autosaved).xlsx).

Project Goals
Uncover True Profitability: Move beyond top-line revenue to identify which specific product categories, regions, and customer segments actively generate net profit versus those that operate at a loss.

Optimize Discounting Strategies: Evaluate the financial impact of current promotional tiers to prevent structural margin leakage.

Monitor Supply Chain Efficiency: Track shipping carrier performance and costs against promised Service Level Agreements (SLAs).

Enable Self-Service Analytics: Provide stakeholders with a dynamic, filterable Excel interface to explore seasonal trends, geographic performance, and customer value without needing external software.

Key Questions Answered by the Dashboard

By navigating the interactive visuals, stakeholders can directly answer the following critical business questions:

Which product categories are our true profit engines?

Identifies that while Furniture drives high revenue, Technology is the sole profit engine, generating 61.5% of total net profit at a 16.03% margin.

At what threshold do discounts begin to destroy value?

Reveals the exact tipping point: discounts above 15% completely destroy value, resulting in an average net loss of $481 per transaction.

Are premium shipping services delivering on their promises?

Highlights operational bottlenecks, showing that the premium "Express Air" service consistently misses its 1-day delivery target, averaging 2.01 days.

Who are our most valuable customers versus our most costly?

Distinguishes between high-revenue customers who actively lose money (due to high discounts and shipping costs) and genuinely high-margin accounts like the top customer, who generated $14,328 in profit from just 5 orders.

How is our sales performance distributed geographically?
Maps out geographic concentration risks, demonstrating that just three states (California, Florida, and New York) account for over 21% of all revenue.
